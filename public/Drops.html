Ramayanam

 

Valmiki Ramayanam was published in 4 parts by Vardhamanan publishers. During this Corona lockdown period, I’m writing here a few of what I learned. Shri. V Jothi M.A., has written all 4 parts in a simple and sweet prose format. I’ve read the translated Tamil version of Ramayanam that was written by the great Thulasidasar in Hindi. Vaanathi publishers published a second version in 1989. In that, a Ramayanam (“Ramacharitha Manas”) written by Goswami Thulasidasar was condensed and clearly written in Tamil by Dr.Shri.T.VenkataKrishnaIyengar who is a professor in English, Tamil and Hindi. Similarly, I’ve also read the “KambaRamayanam” published in 1979 by Kamban Kazhagam. Although this book is in poetry format, meaning and explanations are provided. Apart from these 3 books, Ramayanam has been written in other Indian languages. Since they are written after “KambaRamayanam” and I haven’t learnt other languages, I’m unable to write about any of those in this dissertation. The sole purpose of this dissertation is to showcase to me and my dears to learn that the Poetic Emperor Kambar, in his Ramayanam, he was inspired from the original Valmiki Ramayanam, and he adopted all the incidents without changing the elemental story to Tamil traditions with literary grace.

 

 

Rama’s followers in this world are of 4 types:

1.     Those who wants to know God.

2.     Those who pray God to fulfill their wishes

3.     Those who think about God when they want to wipe out their troubles.

4.     Those who understands God clearly and prays Him.

 

Thulasidasar (Vikrama year BC 1576 – 1631) debuted his Ramayanam (“Ramacharitha Manas”) on a Tuesday (Chithirai month, Sukhla Patcham, Navami) at Ayodhya.

 

At first, Lord Shiva wrote the Ramayanam, which he lectured to the Goddess Parvati. Then, he narrated it to Kagabushundi. Yakngavalkiyar learnt it from Kagabushundi. Yakngavalkiyar narrated it to Bharadhwajar. Thulasidasar heard it from his Gurus at Sukarkhet. Later, he wrote his version of Rama’s history in 7 chapters and he differed in a few instances as compared to the Ramayanam written by Valmiki and Kamban. We shall learn a few of that here.

 

As per Valmiki and Kambar, Vibhishanan is Ravanan’s brother. But Thulasidasar says that Vibhishanan is Ravanan’s step-brother.

 

Yayaathi was cursed by Sukrachariyar to become an old man because he was unloving towards Devayani. Yathu, one of Yayaathi’s sons, denied Yayaathi’s request to accept a portion of the old age curse in exchange for Yathu’s youth. Puru, another son of Yayaathi, accepted to exchange his youth for Yayaathi’s old age curse. Puru was coronated by Yayaathi and Yathu was cursed to parent several demons and live in a city called Krounjam.

 

Rama’s ministers are Jayanthan Vijayan, Sidharthan, Rashtra Vardhanan, Asokan, Dharmabalan Dhrishti, and Sumandhiran.
